About Information

Raigad Fort is a historic hill fortress in Maharashtra's Sahyadri range, located 25 km from Mahad. Established as the Maratha Empire's capital by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1674 AD, this 2,700-foot-high fort features the ruins of the King's Durbar, Ganga Sagar Lake, and the steep Hirakani Buruj, accessible

Raigad is one of the districts in Konkan Division of Maharashtra and Arabian Sea coast forms western boundary of the district. Most hilly places of the District are in the Sahyadri mountain range. Previously known as Kolaba, it was rechristened as Raigad District. Raigad is known for its scintillating historical places, pristine beaches, picturesque landscapes and diverse flora and fauna of Western Ghats. Religious places in the district like Ashtavinayak Temples, Elephanta caves etc. are sole testimonies to its rich cultural past. Culturally, Raigad has been the ever vibrant community with population of different religions, dialects, ethnicity etc. Few places in the Raigad district are historic hinterland of the Bene-Israeli Jews. Raigad has been bestowed with surreal & magnificent forts viz. Raigad Fort, Kolaba Fort, Murud-Janjira Fort, Sudhagad Fort etc. Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Technological University in Raigad, is the only Technological University in the State of Maharashtra. From Industrial Complexes of national importance to the internationally renowned scientific institutions, Raigad is constantly taking strides to the forefronts of 21st century.

Key Information About Raigad Fort:

  • Location: Mahad, Raigad district, Maharashtra.
  • Significance: It was the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, who renovated it and was crowned there in 1674.
  • Altitude: 820 meters (2,700 feet) above sea level.
  • Structure: Built upon a steep rock, it was formerly known as Rairi or Rairee fort.
  • Key Features:
    1. Maha Darwaja: The main entrance to the fort.
    2. King's Durbar: Site of the coronation, featuring a replica of the original throne.
    3. Hirakani Buruj: A famous bastion built on a cliff.
    4. Ganga Sagar Lake: An artificial lake on the fort.

History & Significance

  • Captured: Taken over by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj from the Mores in 1653 CE.
  • Capital: It served as the capital of the Maratha Empire from 1674 CE to 1680 CE.
  • Death of Shivaji: Shivaji Maharaj spent his last days here, passing away in 1680.
  • Best Time to Visit: September to February, as summers are very hot and trekking is difficult in the monsoons.
  • Accessibility: Not accessible by wheelchair.
  • Nearby Attractions: The area is known for its surrounding Sahyadri range views and nearby attractions like the Ashtavinayak temples.
